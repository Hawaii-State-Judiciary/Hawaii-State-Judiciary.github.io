Introduction

Hawaii’s Specialty Courts provide intensive supervision and support to individuals who have not had success on regular probation. The Judiciary has undertaken an initial study using historical data to establish baseline numbers on recidivism and to investigate the effectiveness of these courts. This study investigates patterns of recidivism and probation success with three of the Specialty Courts: Hawaii Drug Court, Mental Health Court, and Veterans Treatment Court.

This report examines rearrests of probationers admitted to a Specialty Court in the First Circuit between 2014 - 2021, following them over a three-year period from the date they entered Specialty Court. The entire cohort is comprised of 539 probationers from the following Specialty Courts:

  1. Hawaii Drug Court – 374
  2. Mental Health Court - 88
  3. Veterans Treatment Court - 77
